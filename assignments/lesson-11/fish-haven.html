Bear Lake History

By Ariana Candez

Fish Haven is located near one of the largest freshwater lakes in the area–Bear Lake. This lake is well-known for its unique shade of blue, caused by limestone depostits within the water. About 109 square miles in size, it sits along the border of Utah and Idaho, split pretty evenly between the two states. The lake was originally discovered by Donald McKenzie, an explorer for the North West Fur Company who discovered the lake in 1819.

As the days warm up with spring right around the corner, many will return to Bear Lake. It has been a popular spot for tourists for some time now, but nobody is as fond of the lake as residents of Fish Haven.
