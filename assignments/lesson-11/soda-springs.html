What Does Equinox Mean?

By Cameron Smith

The spring equinox just passed us--it was March 20th this year. Their are two equinoxes on our calendar, known as the spring equinox and the fall equinox. Many of us understand this as the day the seasons change from winter to spring, and from summer to fall, respectively. But what does 'equinox' even mean?

Equinox refers to the position of the earth in relativeness to the sun. During the quinox, earth's equator is lined up with the center of the sun. It's not exactly the same date every year due to the gravitational pull of the moon and other planets, but it usually happens in March and September. And, though this doesn't technically change anything seasons-wise, we've come to acknowledge this as the first day of the new season. So, for all of you living in the northern hemisphere, happy spring!
