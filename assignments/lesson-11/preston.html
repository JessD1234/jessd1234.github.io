A Bit About Thunderstorms

By Jeremy Blachard

As summer approaches, our thoughts turn to days of warm sunshine and bright blue skies. But that's not all the warm tempuratures bring–we'll also see the return of summer thunderstorms. With this in mind, we did a little research into these exceptionally noisy storms, and thought we'd share these interesting facts:

There are different types of thunderstorms. The storms we usually see are what are known as one-cell storms. They tend to be short, sometimes intense storms, and can trigger others in the area from their leftover energy. There are multi-cell storms, the stronger version of a one-cell storm. These will last longer and usually cover a larger area. The thunderstorms most likely to cause severe weather such as tornados are known as supercell storms. Watch out for those!

Thunderstorms weigh a lot! When we look up at the sky we often don't consider those fluffy cottonballs to weigh much, but according to mentalfloss.com, one cumulus cloud can actually weigh over one million pounds! All that condensed water in one place really weighs a lot!

It's actaully possible to rain animals. Luckily, it's highly unlikely we'll ever see it rain cats and dogs as the old saying goes, but the phrase has some merit. When a tornado passes over a pond or stream, it's possible for small creatures like frogs or fish to be picked up from the water and dropped over our heads later. How weird would that be?
