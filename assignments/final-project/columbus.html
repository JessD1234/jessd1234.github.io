    A Story of Dedication

    The Columbus Ohio temple is the first of the smaller temples we have a location at. Nestled pretty close to the city center in the Dublin area, the temple has a small, well-maintained walkway surrounding it, allowing for a little space away from the business of the world.

    Despite it's small size, this temple is a sign of strength and faith. The saints in Ohio and the surrounding area used to travel to Washington D.C. The drive took around six hours one way. They'd travel in large caravans–whole stakes coming together in caravans to make the trip. Their faith and desire to be sealed to their families encouraged them to make the trip several times a year.

    It was this dedication, and their prayers expressing their desire for a temple of their own, that encouraged the inspiration for President Hinkley to announce a temple in Columbus. One of the first of the smaller temples built, the temple was announced in April of 1998, and dedicated September of 1999. President Hinkley traveled to Columbus to announce and dedicate the temple. Both occasions were a monumental moment for the saints in Ohio.
