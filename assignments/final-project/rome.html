    A Historic Landmark

    Rome is well known for it's place in history–both in the secular world, and in the religious world. In ancient days, Christ's apostles taught the gospel amongst the Romans. After Christ's church disolved, Rome eventually became the headquarters of the Roman Catholic church, one of the oldest known Christian churches.

    Now, Rome is home to 25,956 members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints, as of 2016. The announcement of a temple in this historic location came as a surprise and a long awaited blessing for these saints. Many blessings allowed the temple to be; one of the miracles seen during this time was the inspection of the grounds before it was built. By Italian law, property in Rome must be inspected for Roman ruins before any construction can begin. If any were found, construction would be haulted. The saints in Rome held a special fast during the inspection, and no ruins were found, despite there being an ancient Roman town just 100 yards from the edge of the property.

    The temple was dedicated on March 10, 2019, and began opperation soon after, on March 19. Its interior is marked by many influences of the surrounding city, highlighting the beauty and rich history of Rome.
