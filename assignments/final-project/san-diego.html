Marking the Growth of the Church

The San Diego temple was the 45th temple built. It was dedicated in 1993 by President Gordon B. Hinkley. He dedicated it in a total of twenty-three sessions, where a little over 49,000 people attended. This was the third temple built in California. During its dedication, the church made a great effort to raise awareness of the church in the surrounding area. The resulting event actually won the church an award–the Public Relations Society of America's prestigious Silver Anvil Award in the category of special events and observances by non-profit organizations.

Known for its iconic location along a major freeway, the temple is seen looking over the interstate. This temple has a unique archetectural design not seen in any other temples, and is seen by many as a uniquely beautiful structure. Coupled with the natural beauty of Southern California, this is definitely a temple you don't want to miss.
